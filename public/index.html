Check Exposure

Easy to Use

Allows individuals affected by the virus to upload their location data and members of the community to compare their own location to verify whether they have potentially been exposed to the virus.

User Privacy

Checking if you have been exposed to coronavirus does not need an account. It also does not require you to upload any data to our servers. This means that any sensitive information will stay securely on your own device. If do you choose to help this project by uploading, you are able to exclude any sensitive data, and any data you send will stay anonymous.

Detailed Reporting

Provides a detailed log of possible exposures, taking into account time and location. You can also view locations which have a high rate of coronavirus exposure.

Our Strategy

Data Collection

First, you download location history data from Google Takeout. Assuming you haven't disabled this feature, this file will contain your entire location history since you started your account. As such, it can be rather large. Unzip the file and upload Location History.json.

Data Processing

If data is uploaded to our server, we anonymize it to preserve user privacy, and then store it in a MySQL database. We use an Octree with latitude, longitude, and time as the three dimensions in order to detect collisions.

Data Analysis

Most data analysis is done client side. Your location history is compared with the location history of people who may have coronavirus, and you will be able to explore locations where you may have been exposed. We use OpenStreetMap to graphically visualize results.
